Nikola Tesla

⚡ Biological Path

Born in 1856 in Smiljan, Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor whose life was defined by an obsession with electricity. After immigrating to the U.S. in 1884, he worked briefly with Thomas Edison before striking out on his own.

Despite his genius, he was an eccentric figure who lived most of his life in New York hotels, prioritizing research over wealth. He died in 1943, leaving a legacy that powered the modern world.

🏆 Major Milestones

  • Alternating Current (AC): The global standard for power transmission.
  • The Tesla Coil: Revolutionized wireless and high-frequency research.
  • Radio Principles: Patented the core technologies behind radio transmission.
  • Niagara Falls Plant: Designed the first large-scale hydroelectric power plant.
  • Remote Control: Demonstrated the first radio-guided vessel in 1898.
